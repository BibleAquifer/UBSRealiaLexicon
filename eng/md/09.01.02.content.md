## Sweet wine (id: REALIA:9.1.2)

9\.1\.2 Sweet wine
==================

References:
-----------

Hebrew מַמְתַקִּים (mamthaqim)

[NEH 8:10](https://ref.ly/Neh8:10)

Greek γλεῦκος (gleukos)

[ACT 2:13](https://ref.ly/Acts2:13)

Description:
------------

Sweet wine was wine (see [9\.1 Wine\<REALIA:9\.1\>](#)) to which a sweetening agent, such as honey, had been added.

---

Translation:
------------

[NEH 8:10](https://ref.ly/Neh8:10): Nehemiah orders the people to “eat choice foods and drink sweet drinks” (NJPSV (New Jewish Publication Society Version)). The “sweet drinks” are understood by the Vulgate to be wine sweetened with honey, which is likely (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) “sweet wine”). Like NJPSV (New Jewish Publication Society Version), many other translations have simply “sweet drinks” without specifying the type (NIV (New International Version (1984)), REB (Revised English Bible (1989))).

[ACT 2:13](https://ref.ly/Acts2:13): In classical Greek the word *gleukos* could designate new wine, that is, grape juice in the initial stages of fermentation (or even unfermented grape juice). For this reason some translations have rendered it “new wine” (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952))). However, the chronology of [ACT 2:13](https://ref.ly/Acts2:13) seems to eliminate this possibility. The events described in [ACT 2:0](https://ref.ly/Acts2:0) could not have happened later than the middle of the month of June. This is well before grapes are harvested, and therefore there could have been no fermenting juice. It would have to be from wine from a previous harvest. Those who accused the disciples of being drunk would have known this. While there were methods for keeping such wine sweet, it would not have been “new.” Therefore it seems best to translate *gleukos* here as “sweet wine” or even “sweetened wine.” It is possible, and in many languages preferable, to translate the last clause of this verse as “They have been drinking” (REB (Revised English Bible (1989))) or “These people are drunk” (GNT (Good News Translation (1992))).

* **Associated Passages:** Nehemiah 8:10; Acts 2:13; Acts 2:0
