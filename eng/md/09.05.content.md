## Blocks of pressed dried fruit (id: REALIA:9.5)

9\.5 Blocks of pressed dried fruit
==================================

References:
-----------

Hebrew אָשִׁישׁ, אֲשִׁישָׁה (’ashish, ’ashishah)

[2SA 6:19](https://ref.ly/2Sam6:19), [1CH 16:3](https://ref.ly/1Chr16:3), [SNG 2:5](https://ref.ly/Song2:5), [ISA 16:7](https://ref.ly/Isa16:7), [HOS 3:1](https://ref.ly/Hos3:1)

Hebrew דְּבֵלָה (dvelah)

[1SA 25:18](https://ref.ly/1Sam25:18), [1SA 30:12](https://ref.ly/1Sam30:12), [2KI 20:7](https://ref.ly/2Kgs20:7), [1CH 12:41](https://ref.ly/1Chr12:41), [ISA 38:21](https://ref.ly/Isa38:21)

[JDT 10:5](https://ref.ly/Jdt10:5)

Description:
------------

The fruit of the fig tree was dried in the sun and then pressed together in blocks for storage and for transporting. The same was done with raisins, that is, dried grapes. For blocks of dried fruit used medicinally, see [1\.15\.2 Poultice, fig cake\<REALIA:1\.15\.2\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

For the fig tree and grapevine, see [Fig\<FLORA:2\.6\>](#) and [Vine\<FLORA:2\.12\>](#) respectively. In [1SA 25:18](https://ref.ly/1Sam25:18)GW (God's Word Translation) has “fig cakes,” but this rendering is likely to give the misimpression that these were baked cakes with figs in them. Most translations consulted also use the word “cakes” but in a phrase like “cakes of pressed figs” (NIV (New International Version (1984))), which is slightly better. However, even here the modern reader is most likely to understand “cake” as a baked food. GECL (German Common Language Version (Gute Nachricht Bibel)) avoids this problem with “portions of dried figs,” while CEV (Contemporary English Version) has “handfuls of dried figs.” It will often be possible (and preferable) to say “lumps of pressed figs” or “blocks of dried figs.” Some languages will have a specific word for such blocks of pressed dried fruit.

In [1SA 30:12](https://ref.ly/1Sam30:12)RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) has “a piece of a cake of figs,” but GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) is better with “some dried figs.”
