## Battle-axe (id: REALIA:2.8)

2\.8 Battle\-axe
================

Reference:
----------

Hebrew סגר (sgor)

[PSA 35:3](https://ref.ly/Ps35:3)

Description and usage:
----------------------

![Double-headed battle axe](images/WEB-0385_battle_axe.jpg)

*Double\-headed battle axe (Jebulon, CC0, via Wikimedia Commons)*

The battle\-axe had a handle about half the length of a man’s body on which was mounted a metal blade sharpened on opposing sides. A soldier could use it in close combat and swing it in two directions without changing his grip on it. The illustration shows one possible shape of the blade.

---

Translation:
------------

The Hebrew word *sgor* looks like the imperative form of the verb meaning “close, shut off,” so the Septuagint understood it to mean “capture, make a prisoner.” Many translations take it to be a second weapon with the sword mentioned just before it, and most have rendered it “javelin” (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)), NJPSV (New Jewish Publication Society Version)). However, *A Handbook on Psalms* (page 329\) points out that there is evidence from Qumran that the *sgor* was a kind of axe used in war. Several translations (GNT (Good News Translation (1992)), NASB (New American Standard Bible), ITCL (Italian Common Language Version)) have accepted this meaning. NJB (New Jerusalem Bible (1985)), citing the Qumran information, has “pike,” which was a long thrusting spear. This Handbook recommends “battle\-axe.”

* **Associated Passages:** Psalms 35:3
