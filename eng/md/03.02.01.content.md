## Door of tent, dividing curtain (id: REALIA:3.2.1)

3\.2\.1 Door of tent, dividing curtain
======================================

References:
-----------

Hebrew דֹּק (doq)

[ISA 40:22](https://ref.ly/Isa40:22)

Hebrew יְרִיעָה (yri‘ah)

[PSA 104:2](https://ref.ly/Ps104:2), [SNG 1:5](https://ref.ly/Song1:5), [ISA 54:2](https://ref.ly/Isa54:2), [JER 4:20](https://ref.ly/Jer4:20), [JER 10:20](https://ref.ly/Jer10:20), [JER 49:29](https://ref.ly/Jer49:29), [HAB 3:7](https://ref.ly/Hab3:7)

Greek αὐλαία (aulaia)

[JDT 14:14](https://ref.ly/Jdt14:14)

Description and usage:
----------------------

Curtains divided different parts of a large tent.

---

Translation:
------------

The Hebrew word *yri‘ah* is first used for the cloth strips that covered the Tabernacle (see [3\.15\.2\.3\.6\.1 Linen cloth strips\<REALIA:3\.15\.2\.3\.6\.1\>](#)). In most of the passages listed above this word appears in parallelism with a word meaning “tent,” and for that reason it sometimes seems to be a metonym for tent (compare “the ark of God dwells in the *yri‘ah* ” in [2SA 7:2](https://ref.ly/2Sam7:2) with “the ark of the covenant of the LORD is under a *yri‘ah* ” in [1CH 17:1](https://ref.ly/1Chr17:1)). In [PSA 104:2](https://ref.ly/Ps104:2) the visual imagery “stretched out the heavens like a *yri‘ah* ” comes from the curtains in the Tabernacle stretched out above the ark.

[JDT 14:14](https://ref.ly/Jdt14:14) says “Bagoas went in and knocked at the door of the tent” (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952))). This will sound as strange in some languages as it does in English. NAB (New American Bible (1970)) and NRSV (New Revised Standard Version (1989)) try to solve the problem by changing “door” (*aulaia* in Greek) to “entry.” GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) goes even farther by rendering “Bagoas went in and clapped his hands in front of the sleeping quarters of the tent.” NJB (New Jerusalem Bible (1985)) is closer to the literal meaning of *aulaia*, saying “Bagoas went inside and struck the curtain dividing the tent.” The point of the story is that Bagoas thought Holofernes was in bed with Judith and made noise so as not to surprise them in an indecent position.

* **Associated Passages:** Isaiah 40:22; Psalms 104:2; Song of Songs 1:5; Isaiah 54:2; Jeremiah 4:20; Jeremiah 10:20; Jeremiah 49:29; Habakkuk 3:7; Judith 14:14; 2 Samuel 7:2; 1 Chronicles 17:1
