## Perfumes (id: REALIA:10.6.1)

10\.6\.1 Perfumes
=================

References:

Hebrew רקח, רֶקַח, רֹקַח, רַקָּח, רִקֻּחַ, מִרְקַחַת (raqach (verb), reqach, roqach, riquach, mirqachath)

[EXO 30:25](https://ref.ly/Exod30:25), [EXO 30:25](https://ref.ly/Exod30:25), [EXO 30:25](https://ref.ly/Exod30:25), [EXO 30:35](https://ref.ly/Exod30:35), [EXO 30:35](https://ref.ly/Exod30:35), [EXO 37:29](https://ref.ly/Exod37:29), [1SA 8:13](https://ref.ly/1Sam8:13), [1CH 9:30](https://ref.ly/1Chr9:30), [1CH 9:30](https://ref.ly/1Chr9:30), [2CH 16:14](https://ref.ly/2Chr16:14), [2CH 16:14](https://ref.ly/2Chr16:14), [NEH 3:8](https://ref.ly/Neh3:8), [ECC 10:1](https://ref.ly/Eccl10:1), [SNG 8:2](https://ref.ly/Song8:2), [ISA 57:9](https://ref.ly/Isa57:9)

Hebrew שֶׁמֶן (shemen)

[2SA 14:2](https://ref.ly/2Sam14:2), [PRO 27:9](https://ref.ly/Prov27:9), [ECC 7:1](https://ref.ly/Eccl7:1), [ECC 9:8](https://ref.ly/Eccl9:8), [ECC 10:1](https://ref.ly/Eccl10:1), [SNG 1:3](https://ref.ly/Song1:3), [SNG 1:3](https://ref.ly/Song1:3), [SNG 4:10](https://ref.ly/Song4:10), [ISA 57:9](https://ref.ly/Isa57:9), [ISA 61:3](https://ref.ly/Isa61:3), [EZK 16:9](https://ref.ly/Ezek16:9), [MIC 6:15](https://ref.ly/Mic6:15)

Greek ἤδυσμα (ēdusma)

[ESG 4:17](https://ref.ly/EsthGr4:17)

Description:
------------

Perfumes were specially produced mixtures of pleasant\-smelling spices usually added to an oil base. They were used to cover unpleasant smells such as human perspiration, decaying bodies, or even the bloody results of ritual slaughter.

---

Translation:
------------

The Hebrew verb *raqach* means “to mix, compound,” while the Hebrew word *shemen* means “oil.”

* **Associated Passages:** Exodus 30:25; Exodus 30:35; Exodus 37:29; 1 Samuel 8:13; 1 Chronicles 9:30; 2 Chronicles 16:14; Nehemiah 3:8; Ecclesiastes 10:1; Song of Songs 8:2; Isaiah 57:9; 2 Samuel 14:2; Proverbs 27:9; Ecclesiastes 7:1; Ecclesiastes 9:8; Song of Songs 1:3; Song of Songs 4:10; Isaiah 61:3; Ezekiel 16:9; Micah 6:15; Esther Greek 4:17


## Myrrh (id: REALIA:10.6.1.1)

10\.6\.1\.1 Myrrh
=================

References:
-----------

Hebrew לֹט (lot)

[GEN 37:25](https://ref.ly/Gen37:25), [GEN 43:11](https://ref.ly/Gen43:11)

Hebrew מֹר (mor)

[EXO 30:23](https://ref.ly/Exod30:23), [EST 2:12](https://ref.ly/Esth2:12), [PSA 45:9](https://ref.ly/Ps45:9), [PRO 7:17](https://ref.ly/Prov7:17), [SNG 1:13](https://ref.ly/Song1:13), [SNG 3:6](https://ref.ly/Song3:6), [SNG 4:6](https://ref.ly/Song4:6), [SNG 4:14](https://ref.ly/Song4:14), [SNG 5:1](https://ref.ly/Song5:1), [SNG 5:5](https://ref.ly/Song5:5), [SNG 5:5](https://ref.ly/Song5:5), [SNG 5:13](https://ref.ly/Song5:13)

[MAT 26:7](https://ref.ly/Matt26:7), [MAT 26:12](https://ref.ly/Matt26:12), [MRK 14:3](https://ref.ly/Mark14:3), [MRK 14:4](https://ref.ly/Mark14:4), [LUK 7:38](https://ref.ly/Luke7:38), [LUK 7:46](https://ref.ly/Luke7:46), [JHN 11:2](https://ref.ly/John11:2), [JHN 12:3](https://ref.ly/John12:3), [JHN 12:3](https://ref.ly/John12:3), [JHN 12:5](https://ref.ly/John12:5), [REV 18:13](https://ref.ly/Rev18:13), [JDT 10:3](https://ref.ly/Jdt10:3), [WIS 2:7](https://ref.ly/Wis2:7)

Greek σμύρνα, σμυρνίζω (smurna, smurnizō (verb))

[MAT 2:11](https://ref.ly/Matt2:11), [MRK 15:23](https://ref.ly/Mark15:23), [SIR 24:15](https://ref.ly/Sir24:15)

Description and usage:
----------------------

Myrrh was the aromatic resin of certain bushes. This resin was added to an oil base to make a perfume to put on the body. See also [4\.8\.3\.1 Embalming oils\<REALIA:4\.8\.3\.1\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

The tree that produced the myrrh resin was not native to Israel, and the import of myrrh made it an expensive luxury. This should be borne in mind when translating [EST 2:12](https://ref.ly/Esth2:12); [PSA 45:9](https://ref.ly/Ps45:9); [PRO 7:17](https://ref.ly/Prov7:17); [SNG 1:13](https://ref.ly/Song1:13); [MAT 2:11](https://ref.ly/Matt2:11).

Most translators borrow a term for “myrrh.” In some passages translators may expand the translation to indicate the nature of the substance; compare GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) at [PSA 45:9](https://ref.ly/Ps45:9), where it says “perfume of myrrh.” In many instances, particularly in the New Testament, they may use a general expression such as “ointment,” “aromatic oil,” or “perfume.” When the Greek word *muron* means “perfume,” it may be rendered “sweet\-smelling oil” or “fragrant oil.”

* **Associated Passages:** Genesis 37:25; Genesis 43:11; Exodus 30:23; Esther 2:12; Psalms 45:9; Proverbs 7:17; Song of Songs 1:13; Song of Songs 3:6; Song of Songs 4:6; Song of Songs 4:14; Song of Songs 5:1; Song of Songs 5:5; Song of Songs 5:13; 3 Maccabees 4:6; Matthew 26:7; Matthew 26:12; Mark 14:3; Mark 14:4; Luke 7:38; Luke 7:46; John 11:2; John 12:3; John 12:5; Revelation 18:13; Judith 10:3; Wisdom of Solomon 2:7; Matthew 2:11; Mark 15:23; Sirach 24:15

* **Associated ACAI Concepts:** Myrrh (ID: `flora:Myrrh`); Myrrh (ID: `realia:Myrrh`)

## Oil of nard (id: REALIA:10.6.1.2)

10\.6\.1\.2 Oil of nard
=======================

References:
-----------

Hebrew נֵרְדְּ (nerd)

[SNG 1:12](https://ref.ly/Song1:12), [SNG 4:13](https://ref.ly/Song4:13), [SNG 4:14](https://ref.ly/Song4:14)

Greek νάρδος (nardos)

[MRK 14:3](https://ref.ly/Mark14:3), [JHN 12:3](https://ref.ly/John12:3)

Description:
------------

Oil of nard was an aromatic oil extracted from a plant called nard.

---

Translation:
------------

Translators normally borrow the term “nard” but use some type of classifier, for example, “perfume called nard” or “sweet\-smelling substance, nard.” For other issues related to the above verses, see also [5\.18\.1\.3 Alabaster jar, alabaster flask\<REALIA:5\.18\.1\.3\>](#).

* **Associated Passages:** Song of Songs 1:12; Song of Songs 4:13; Song of Songs 4:14; Mark 14:3; John 12:3

* **Associated ACAI Concepts:** Nard (ID: `flora:Nard`); Oil of Nard (ID: `realia:OilOfNard`)
