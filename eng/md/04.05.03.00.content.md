Description:
------------

The robe of the High Priest was worn on top of the tunic and thus was a kind of jacket or overshirt. It was woven of light blue wool thread. It was a long garment, hanging down to the ankles. It is not clear whether it had sleeves or not, nor whether it was open or closed in the front. Somewhat like a Latin American poncho, it may even have been a rectangular piece of cloth with a hole in the middle for the head. Around the hem of the robe were sewn alternating golden bells (see [4\.5\.3\.1 Jinglet, bell\<REALIA:4\.5\.3\.1\>](#)) and pomegranates made of woven threads. The robe was held in place by the ephod, which was worn over it. See the illustrations at [4\.5 High Priest’s clothing\<REALIA:4\.5\>](#) and [4\.5\.4 Ephod\<REALIA:4\.5\.4\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

This garment is sometimes called “the robe of the ephod” (so RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) in [EXO 28:31](https://ref.ly/Exod28:31); [EXO 29:5](https://ref.ly/Exod29:5); [EXO 39:22](https://ref.ly/Exod39:22)). This means simply that the ephod was worn over it.
