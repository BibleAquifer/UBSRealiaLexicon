Description:
------------

![Sheaves of grain from the harvest](../json/images/WEB-0934_sheaf.jpg)

*Sheaves of grain from the harvest ( Jakub Hałun © Wikimedia Commons)*

The sheaf was a bundle made by binding together the stalks and ears of a crop that had been harvested. The binding was most often done with stalks of the grain itself.

---

Usage:
------

Grain was harvested by cutting the stalks low to the ground with a sickle (see [1\.1\.6 Sickle\<REALIA:1\.1\.6\>](#)) or even by pulling them up by hand. The stalks were then gathered into bundles and tied together around the middle, making a sheaf. If the sheaves were not collected immediately, they could be left in the field in a standing position to keep the heads of grain off the damp ground. After the sheaves were collected, they were taken to the threshing floor (see [1\.1\.8\.1 Threshing floor\<REALIA:1\.1\.8\.1\>](#)).

---

Translation:
------------

For the word “sheaf,” translators may use a descriptive phrase, such as “bundle of harvested grain.” In some cultures the practice of tying grain into bundles is unknown. Some languages may have to say something like “bunch of grain you tie together.” In some cases it will be necessary to specify what kind of grain is involved. It may be possible to render “sheaf” in a more general sense as “harvest” (so GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) in [PSA 126:6](https://ref.ly/Ps126:6); compare GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) in [JOB 24:10](https://ref.ly/Job24:10), where it has “wheat”) or even “grain” (so GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) in [AMO 2:13](https://ref.ly/Amos2:13) and [MIC 4:12](https://ref.ly/Mic4:12)). Another passage where GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) renders “sheaf” in a broader sense is [JDT 8:3](https://ref.ly/Jdt8:3). The literal RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) text has “as he stood overseeing the men who were binding sheaves in the field,” but GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) says “while in the fields supervising the farm workers.”

The Hebrew words *‘amir* and *‘omer* actually indicate a heap of many sheaves gathered at the edge of the field before being transported to the threshing floor, so the focus of [AMO 2:13](https://ref.ly/Amos2:13) is on the collective weight of the grain.
