## Buckle, brooch, clasp (id: REALIA:6.2.3)

6\.2\.3 Buckle, brooch, clasp
=============================

References:
-----------

Hebrew חָח (chach)

[EXO 35:22](https://ref.ly/Exod35:22)

Greek πόρπη (porpē)

[1MA 10:89](https://ref.ly/1Macc10:89), [1MA 11:58](https://ref.ly/1Macc11:58), [1MA 14:44](https://ref.ly/1Macc14:44)

Description and usage:
----------------------

![Robe pin (fibula)](../json/images/WEB-0502_robe_pin.jpg)

*Robe pin (fibula) (© Marcus Cyron, CC BY\-SA 3\.0, via Wikimedia Commons)*

The buckle was a kind of clasp or pin used to fasten a cape or robe at the shoulder. It served as a symbol of rank, and the wearing of it was granted as a privilege.

---

Translation:
------------

Some cultures may have a special symbol worn by certain privileged or honored persons. It may be a piece of jewelry worn on the clothing, a necklace, a special headgear, or something else. It may be possible to use such a word in all three references for the Greek word *porpē*.

The Hebrew word *chach* in [EXO 35:22](https://ref.ly/Exod35:22) refers to some kind of jewelry, perhaps a brooch or even a kind of ring (see [10\.5\.1 Earring, nose ring\<REALIA:10\.5\.1\>](#)). In other contexts it refers to a device placed in the nose of a prisoner by which he was led around against his will (see [1\.3\.3 Fishing spear, harpoon\<REALIA:1\.3\.3\>](#)).

* **Associated Passages:** Exodus 35:22; 1 Maccabees 10:89; 1 Maccabees 11:58; 1 Maccabees 14:44
