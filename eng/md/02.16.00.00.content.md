## Wooden tower on elephant (id: REALIA:2.16)

2\.16 Wooden tower on elephant
==============================

Reference:
----------

Greek πύργος, ξύλινος (purgos xulinos)

[1MA 6:37](https://ref.ly/1Macc6:37)

Description and usage:
----------------------

![War elephants](images/WEB-0393_war_elephants.jpg)

*War elephants (Bernard Picart, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons)*

In ancient warfare, elephants were sometimes used almost like a tank in modern warfare. The animal was so large that it frightened opposing soldiers and could hardly be withstood by an individual soldier. Sometimes a kind of wooden tower or box was placed on the back of the elephant as an elevated firing platform for soldiers. The whole heavy apparatus was strapped on the elephant with straps (called *mēchanē* in this verse) that went under the animal’s belly and perhaps around its neck and even under its tail. NJB (New Jerusalem Bible (1985)) renders these straps as “girths.”

---

Translation:
------------

In [1MA 6:37](https://ref.ly/1Macc6:37) the actual number of soldiers on each platform is in doubt. The Greek text has “thirty” or “thirty\-two,” which would be too many even for a large elephant. Rahlfs emends the text to read “four” (followed by RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952))), while GNT (Good News Translation (1992)), NJB (New Jerusalem Bible (1985)), and TOB (Traduction Oecuménique de la Bible (French, 1975)) have “three.” The Norwegian Bible (NOB) has “two.” The normal number of soldiers on an elephant, according to Charles, was three or four, and translators will normally have one of these numbers and perhaps include a footnote.

* **Associated Passages:** 1 Maccabees 6:37
