5\.16 Basin, washbasin

The basin was a large bowl, normally made of clay. It had several uses; commonly it held water and was used for washing the hands, face, and feet.

---

Translation:
------------

[JHN 13:5](https://ref.ly/John13:5): The word *niptēr* does not appear elsewhere in Greek literature; its meaning is uncertain, but most translations have “basin,” “washbasin,” or “bowl.” It can be argued that the word means “pitcher” or “jug,” since in the Ancient Near East, people’s feet were not generally washed in a basin of standing water but by pouring water over the feet from a pitcher. With this understanding the picture in [JHN 13:5](https://ref.ly/John13:5) would be as follows: The disciples were on cushions, reclining on their left sides, and using their right hands to reach into the dishes that were on the table, or tables, placed in front of them. Jesus poured water into “the pitcher” (the Greek text has the definite article “the,” suggesting that it was a particular vessel used specifically for that purpose) and then went around behind the disciples, where their feet were stretched out behind them. There he poured water from the pitcher over their feet and dried them with the towel (see [10\.4 Towel\<REALIA:10\.4\>](#)) wrapped around his waist.
