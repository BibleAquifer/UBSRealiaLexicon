Description and usage:
----------------------

![Lampstands](images/UBS-0029_lampstand.jpg)

*Lampstands (© Deutsche Bibelgesellschaft, Stuttgart by United Bible Societies)*

The lampstand was a stand designed to hold a single lamp or a series of lamps. It could be made of a variety of materials and took various shapes and sizes. Its purpose was to raise the light higher than the level of the floor or table and so light a larger area. For the lampstand in the Tabernacle, see [4\.3\.4 Lampstand, menorah\<REALIA:4\.3\.4\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

![Oil lamps](images/WEB-0941_oil_lamps.jpg)

*Oil lamps (© Zde Wikimedia Commons)*

Since there is no special word for “lampstand” in many cultures, this may have to be translated “lamp holder,” “thing on which lamps are placed,” or “place for putting lamps.” Where the translator has chosen to render the oil lamp as “candle,” the appropriate word for a candleholder could be used.

[DAN 5:5](https://ref.ly/Dan5:5): The writing on the wall is said to have been “opposite the lampstand” (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952))). This would be in the most clearly illuminated part of the banquet hall. This should probably be stated clearly in most languages. GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) has “where the light from the lamps was shining most brightly.” Also possible is “where the light was strongest” or “where people could see very clearly.”
