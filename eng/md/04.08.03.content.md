## Embalming oils (id: REALIA:4.8.3.1)

4\.8\.3\.1 Embalming oils
=========================

References:
-----------

Greek ἀλόη (aloē)

[JHN 19:39](https://ref.ly/John19:39)

Greek ἄρωμα (arōma)

[MRK 16:1](https://ref.ly/Mark16:1), [LUK 23:56](https://ref.ly/Luke23:56), [LUK 24:1](https://ref.ly/Luke24:1), [JHN 19:40](https://ref.ly/John19:40)

Greek μύρον (muron)

[LUK 23:56](https://ref.ly/Luke23:56)

Greek σμύρνα (smurna)

[JHN 19:39](https://ref.ly/John19:39)

Description and usage:
----------------------

Embalming oils were aromatic oils or salves used especially in preparing a dead body for burial in a way that it would be preserved. See also [10\.6\.1\.1 Myrrh\<REALIA:10\.6\.1\.1\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

In [2CH 16:14](https://ref.ly/2Chr16:14) we find the Hebrew expression *bsamim uznim mruqachim* in the context of the burial of King Asa of Judah. The text says that spices and aromatic oils were placed in the tomb (CEV (Contemporary English Version) “the tomb was filled with spices and sweet\-smelling oils”). There is no reason to assume that Asa’s body was embalmed (like Jacob’s body in [GEN 50:2](https://ref.ly/Gen50:2); [GEN 50:3](https://ref.ly/Gen50:3)), although GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) comes close with “They used spices and perfumed oils to prepare his body for burial.”

In [LUK 23:56](https://ref.ly/Luke23:56) translators may use a descriptive phrase to render the Greek word *muron*, for example, “perfumed oil for preserving the body” or “… to keep the body from rotting.”

Ingredients mentioned in the New Testament for embalming oils are “myrrh” (*smurna* in Greek) and “aloes” (*aloē* in Greek). Most translators borrow terms for these ingredients and then use some kind of marginal note to explain the nature of their substance.

* **Associated Passages:** John 19:39; Mark 16:1; Luke 23:56; Luke 24:1; John 19:40; 2 Chronicles 16:14; Genesis 50:2; Genesis 50:3
