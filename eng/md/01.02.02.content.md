## Animal pen, stall (id: REALIA:1.2.2)

1\.2\.2 Animal pen, stall
=========================

References:
-----------

Hebrew אֻרְוָה (’awerah, ’urwah, ’uryah)

[1KI 5:6](https://ref.ly/1Kgs5:6), [2CH 9:25](https://ref.ly/2Chr9:25), [2CH 32:28](https://ref.ly/2Chr32:28)

Hebrew בַּיִת (bayith)

[1SA 6:7](https://ref.ly/1Sam6:7), [1SA 6:10](https://ref.ly/1Sam6:10)

Hebrew מַרְבֵּק (marbeq)

[1SA 28:24](https://ref.ly/1Sam28:24), [JER 46:21](https://ref.ly/Jer46:21), [AMO 6:4](https://ref.ly/Amos6:4), [MAL 3:20](https://ref.ly/Mal3:20)

Hebrew רֶפֶת (refeth)

[HAB 3:17](https://ref.ly/Hab3:17)

Greek φάτνη (fatnē)

[LUK 13:15](https://ref.ly/Luke13:15)

Description and usage:
----------------------

This structure was a stall, part of a barn where animals were fed, or it was simply a customary place where animals were fed outside, often in a semi\-circular enclosure. Since people normally possessed few cattle, the stalls were often near, or even inside, the house where the family lived.

---

Translation:
------------

In contrast to [1\.2\.1 Sheep pen, sheepfold\<REALIA:1\.2\.1\>](#), this enclosure was permanent and built near the dwelling of the owner of the sheep.

In [1SA 28:24](https://ref.ly/1Sam28:24); [JER 46:21](https://ref.ly/Jer46:21); and [AMO 6:4](https://ref.ly/Amos6:4), the Hebrew phrase *‘egel marbeq* is literally “calf of the stall,” indicating a calf that has been kept in a stall and fed until it is fat. While *marbeq* may usually be left untranslated, it is also possible to follow CEV (Contemporary English Version) ’s translation of the equivalent phrase in [LUK 15:23](https://ref.ly/Luke15:23), where it has “best calf” (compare GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) “prize calf”).
