## Pēchus (id: REALIA:11.4.2.1)

11\.4\.2\.1 Pēchus
==================

References:
-----------

Greek πῆχυς (pēchus)

[MAT 6:27](https://ref.ly/Matt6:27), [LUK 12:25](https://ref.ly/Luke12:25), [JHN 21:8](https://ref.ly/John21:8), [REV 21:17](https://ref.ly/Rev21:17), [JDT 1:2](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:2), [JDT 1:2](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:2), [JDT 1:2](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:2), [JDT 1:2](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:2), [JDT 1:3](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:3), [JDT 1:3](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:3), [JDT 1:4](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:4), [JDT 1:4](https://ref.ly/Jdt1:4), [2MA 13:5](https://ref.ly/2Macc13:5), [1ES 6:24](https://ref.ly/1Esd6:24), [1ES 6:24](https://ref.ly/1Esd6:24), [S3Y 1:24](https://ref.ly/PrAzar1:24)

The *pēchus* is the New Testament equivalent of the cubit (*’amah* in Hebrew).
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In most cases the translator will be able to avoid awkward fractions and decimal points for the *pēchus* by using the formula, two cubits equal one meter or one yard.

[MAT 6:27](https://ref.ly/Matt6:27); [LUK 12:25](https://ref.ly/Luke12:25): One problem of interpretation here is that the Greek word *pēchus* is normally used of distance rather than of time. However, it may also be used of time, and that meaning is much more satisfactory in the present context. Moreover, the Greek word rendered “span of life” by RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) is more frequently used as a measurement of time than height. Modern translations tend to accept the meaning of a measure of time. If span is used as a measurement of time, the use of the word *pēchus* to qualify it would mean a brief span, whereas if it is used of a person’s height, it would be a significant amount (about half a meter or 18 inches). NAB (New American Bible (1970)) renders the last part of [MAT 6:27](https://ref.ly/Matt6:27) as “add a moment to his life\-span,” and GECL (German Common Language Version (Gute Nachricht Bibel)) has “lengthen his life only by one day.” Other possibilities are “add a little time to his life,” “live a little longer,” and “make his life last even a little longer.” Some translators will indicate a specific amount of time, such as “even one day” or “even one hour.”

[JHN 21:8](https://ref.ly/John21:8): The distance given here is approximately 100 meters or 100 yards. Translators will want to follow something like CEV (Contemporary English Version) “only about a hundred yards from shore.”

[REV 21:17](https://ref.ly/Rev21:17): The text gives a measurement of 144 cubits, that is, twelve times twelve. This seems to be a symbolic number, and many translations—among them common\-language translations such as GW (God's Word Translation)NCV (New Century Version)ITCL (Italian Common Language Version)GECL (German Common Language Version (Gute Nachricht Bibel)) —choose to leave the number in the text. ITCL (Italian Common Language Version) also adds the metric equivalent in the text, saying “one hundred forty\-four cubits (seventy meters).”

GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) takes this measurement, 144 cubits (72 meters; 236 feet), to be the height of the wall (also NJB (New Jerusalem Bible (1985))REB (Revised English Bible (1989))FRCL (French Common Language Version (Bible en français courant)), Phps (J.B. Phillips: The New Testament in Modern English (1958))). It may, however, refer to its thickness (NIV (New International Version (1984))BRCL (Brazilian Common Language Bible)). Some commentators make the point that, for a city that is 1,500 miles tall, a wall only 72 meters (236 feet) tall is so small that it seems foolish. But it is also pointed out that the wall, in this case, is not for the protection of the city (inasmuch as the gates stay open all the time) but for its demarcation. In any case, the notion of height is preferable to that of thickness. A translation should opt for one or the other; simply to say, as RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) and others say, “a hundred and forty\-four cubits” (or, “72 meters” or “236 feet”), without indicating that this is the height, does not qualify as a translation. An alternative translation model for the first half of this verse is “He also determined the height \[or, thickness] of the wall \[or, fence]. It was 72 meters \[or, 236 feet].” For this verse see also *A Handbook on The Revelation to John*, pages 306–307\.
