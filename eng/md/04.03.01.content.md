## Tabernacle: Washstand, basin, bowl (id: REALIA:4.3.1)

4\.3\.1 Tabernacle: Washstand, basin, bowl
==========================================

References:
-----------

Hebrew כִּיּוֹר (kiyor)

[EXO 30:18](https://ref.ly/Exod30:18), [EXO 30:28](https://ref.ly/Exod30:28), [EXO 31:9](https://ref.ly/Exod31:9), [EXO 35:16](https://ref.ly/Exod35:16), [EXO 38:8](https://ref.ly/Exod38:8), [EXO 39:39](https://ref.ly/Exod39:39), [EXO 40:7](https://ref.ly/Exod40:7), [EXO 40:11](https://ref.ly/Exod40:11), [EXO 40:30](https://ref.ly/Exod40:30), [LEV 8:11](https://ref.ly/Lev8:11)

Description and usage:
----------------------

The basin was a large copper pot that stood on a copper base. It was used by the priests to wash their hands and feet. It was probably open at the top for filling with water and may have had spouts near the bottom to allow the water to run out. According to [EXO 30:18](https://ref.ly/Exod30:18), it stood “between the tent of meeting and the altar” (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952))).

---

Translation:
------------

The priests were commanded to wash their hands and feet before beginning their daily work. This was a ceremonial act of purification for which the basin held the water.

The word “laver,” used by some English translations (for example, RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952)) and NJPSV (New Jewish Publication Society Version)), is obscure and communicates little to the average reader. NCV (New Century Version) “bowl” is likely to be perceived as something much smaller than its actual size. It is better to say “large bowl” (so CEV (Contemporary English Version)) or “large basin.”

* **Associated Passages:** Exodus 30:18; Exodus 30:28; Exodus 31:9; Exodus 35:16; Exodus 38:8; Exodus 39:39; Exodus 40:7; Exodus 40:11; Exodus 40:30; Leviticus 8:11
