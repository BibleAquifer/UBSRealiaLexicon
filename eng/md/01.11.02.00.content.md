## Anvil (id: REALIA:1.11.2)

1\.11\.2 Anvil
==============

References:
-----------

Hebrew פַּעַם (pa‘am)

[ISA 41:7](https://ref.ly/Isa41:7)

Greek ἄκμων (akmōn)

[SIR 38:28](https://ref.ly/Sir38:28)

Description and usage:
----------------------

![Modern anvil](../json/images/WEB-0369_anvil.jpg)

*Modern anvil (© MikiNikoloski, CC BY\-SA 3\.0, via Wikimedia Commons)*

The anvil was a heavy piece of metal that served as a base for hammering and shaping other pieces of metal.

---

Translation:
------------

The meaning of the Hebrew phrase *holem pa‘am* in [ISA 41:7](https://ref.ly/Isa41:7) is uncertain, so translations vary. The basic meaning seems to be connected to striking blows with a hammer. This has given rise to several interpretations: GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) “the one who nails it together,” NCV (New Century Version) “the one who shapes the metal,” and NRSV (New Revised Standard Version (1989)) “the one who strikes the anvil” (similarly NIV (New International Version (1984))). CEV (Contemporary English Version) has “other workers” and adds a footnote explaining that the text is difficult.

Where there is no equivalent for “anvil,” in [SIR 38:28](https://ref.ly/Sir38:28) translators may say “It is the same with the iron worker at his work.”
