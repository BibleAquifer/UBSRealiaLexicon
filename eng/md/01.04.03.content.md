## Cage (id: REALIA:1.4.3)

1\.4\.3 Cage
============

References:
-----------

Hebrew סוּגַר (sugar)

[EZK 19:9](https://ref.ly/Ezek19:9)

Greek κάρταλλος (kartallos)

[SIR 11:30](https://ref.ly/Sir11:30)

Description and usage:
----------------------

![Doves in a cage, awaiting to be sold in the temple and to be used as sacrifice](../json/images/OAI-0001_dovesincage.png)

*Doves in a cage, awaiting to be sold in the temple and to be used as sacrifice (Image generated by ChatGPT using OpenAI technology)*

The cage was a kind of basket, woven loosely of cane or sticks. It had a top or door that could be closed. When a bird or other prey was caught alive, it was confined in the cage. Sometimes the basket itself served as a kind of trap.

---

Translation:
------------

The Hebrew word *sugar* in [EZK 19:9](https://ref.ly/Ezek19:9) probably refers to a kind of wooden collar put on prisoners (see [1\.1\.1 Yoke\<REALIA:1\.1\.1\>](#)). However, perhaps because the prophet pictures Judah’s king as a captured lion here, most translations render it “cage.” A few translations (GECL (German Common Language Version (Gute Nachricht Bibel)), ITCL (Italian Common Language Version)) have “collar” or “yoke.” NJB (New Jerusalem Bible (1985)) has “shackled.”

Sometimes a captive bird could be used as a lure to capture a larger animal. The bird was tied inside the basket. When the prey entered the basket to take the bait, the basket was closed upon it by some device. It is presumably this interpretation of [SIR 11:30](https://ref.ly/Sir11:30) that has caused GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) to render the first half of this verse as “A proud person is a decoy to lure you into danger.” Other translations omit the element of the lure; for example, the New American Bible (NAB (New American Bible (1970))) has “Though he seem like a bird confined in a cage” (similarly ITCL (Italian Common Language Version)).
