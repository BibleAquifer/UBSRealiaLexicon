## Casting net (id: REALIA:1.3.1.1)

1\.3\.1\.1 Casting net
======================

References:
-----------

Hebrew מִכְמָר, מִכְמֶרֶת, מִכְמֹרֶת (mikmor, mikmereth, mikmoreth)

[PSA 141:10](https://ref.ly/Ps141:10), [ISA 19:8](https://ref.ly/Isa19:8), [HAB 1:15](https://ref.ly/Hab1:15), [HAB 1:16](https://ref.ly/Hab1:16)

Hebrew רֶשֶׁת (resheth)

[EZK 32:3](https://ref.ly/Ezek32:3)

Greek ἀμφιβάλλω, ἀμφίβληστρον (amfiballō (verb), amfiblēstron)

[MAT 4:18](https://ref.ly/Matt4:18), [MRK 1:16](https://ref.ly/Mark1:16)

Greek δίκτυον (diktuon)

[JHN 21:6](https://ref.ly/John21:6), [JHN 21:8](https://ref.ly/John21:8), [JHN 21:11](https://ref.ly/John21:11), [JHN 21:11](https://ref.ly/John21:11)

Description:
------------

![Fisherman casting his net from a boat](../json/images/WEB-0958_fishermen_net.jpg)

*Fisherman casting his net from a boat (© Free Bible Images © David Padfield)*

The casting net was a circular net with weights and a draw rope around its edge. It was roughly 6–7\.5 meters (20–25 feet) in diameter.

---

Usage:
------

![Fisherman pulling up a catch of fish in a net](../json/images/WEB-0959_fishermen_net.jpg)

*Fisherman pulling up a catch of fish in a net (© Free Bible Images © David Padfield)*

The net was thrown from a boat or the shore in a circular motion so that it landed with the net fully spread out on the water. The weights carried it down, trapping fish underneath. Then the fisherman used the draw rope to close the net and pull it back to himself.

---

Translation:
------------

It is not necessary to express all the details of the “casting net” in order to render it adequately in another language. What is important is that such a net was thrown rather than dragged. A generic term for “net” may be used in languages that do not have distinct terms for different kinds of nets.

The Hebrew word *resheth* normally refers to a hunting net for animals or birds (see [1\.4\.2 Net\<REALIA:1\.4\.2\>](#)). In [EZK 32:3](https://ref.ly/Ezek32:3) it is used in parallel with the Hebrew word *cherem*, which means “dragnet” (see [1\.3\.1\.2 Dragnet, seine\<REALIA:1\.3\.1\.2\>](#)). The Hebrew verb that accompanies *resheth* means “throw” or “spread out,” the action associated with the casting net, and for that reason it is included here. Most translations agree that the context of this verse is fishing and not hunting. However, most translations choose a general word for “net” rather than trying to specify which type of fishing net is intended.

[HAB 1:15](https://ref.ly/Hab1:15); [HAB 1:16](https://ref.ly/Hab1:16): Two different words for net are used in these verses (see [1\.3\.1\.2 Dragnet, seine\<REALIA:1\.3\.1\.2\>](#) for a discussion on the other type of net). If different kinds of fishing nets are familiar in a culture, translators will have no difficulty in speaking about them. If they are not, then it will probably be best to use one generic term for both types of fishing nets here (so GNT (Good News Translation (1992)) “nets”).

[JHN 21:3](https://ref.ly/John21:3): When Peter says “I am going fishing” (GNT (Good News Translation (1992))), some languages will need to make clear what kind of fishing is intended, whether, for example, Peter will be using a hook and line, a trap, or a net. The most normal method of fishing in biblical times was with nets, and this is the meaning here.

* **Associated Passages:** Psalms 141:10; Isaiah 19:8; Habakkuk 1:15; Habakkuk 1:16; Ezekiel 32:3; Matthew 4:18; Mark 1:16; John 21:6; John 21:8; John 21:11; John 21:3


## Dragnet, seine (id: REALIA:1.3.1.2)

1\.3\.1\.2 Dragnet, seine
=========================

References:
-----------

Hebrew חֵרֶם (cherem)

[EZK 26:5](https://ref.ly/Ezek26:5), [EZK 26:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek26:14), [EZK 32:3](https://ref.ly/Ezek32:3), [EZK 47:10](https://ref.ly/Ezek47:10), [HAB 1:15](https://ref.ly/Hab1:15), [HAB 1:16](https://ref.ly/Hab1:16), [HAB 1:17](https://ref.ly/Hab1:17)

Greek σαγήνη (sagēnē)

[MAT 13:47](https://ref.ly/Matt13:47)

Description:
------------

![Men drawing in a dragnet](../json/images/WEB-0335_drag_net.png)

*Men drawing in a dragnet (George Louis Faber, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons)*

The dragnet was a long net hanging vertically in the water with floats at the upper edge and weights at the lower edge.

---

Usage:
------

The dragnet was drawn in by men working in boats or from shore. As the ends were drawn together to the boat or drawn ashore, fish were trapped in the area enclosed by the net.

---

Translation:
------------

A generic term for “net” may be used in languages that do not require that a distinction be made.

In [MAT 13:47](https://ref.ly/Matt13:47) the actual form of the net is not so important. What is significant is that the net was sufficiently large to catch a large number of different kinds of fish.

[HAB 1:15](https://ref.ly/Hab1:15); [HAB 1:16](https://ref.ly/Hab1:16); [HAB 1:17](https://ref.ly/Hab1:17): See the comments at [1\.3\.1\.1 Casting net\<REALIA:1\.3\.1\.1\>](#).

* **Associated Passages:** Ezekiel 26:5; Ezekiel 26:14; Ezekiel 32:3; Ezekiel 47:10; Habakkuk 1:15; Habakkuk 1:16; Habakkuk 1:17; Matthew 13:47


## Net, trammel net (id: REALIA:1.3.1.3)

1\.3\.1\.3 Net, trammel net
===========================

References:
-----------

Hebrew מָצוֹד (matsod (see also )

[JOB 19:6](https://ref.ly/Job19:6), [ECC 7:26](https://ref.ly/Eccl7:26)

Hebrew מְצוֹדָה (mtsodah)

[ECC 9:12](https://ref.ly/Eccl9:12)

Greek δίκτυον (diktuon)

[MAT 4:20](https://ref.ly/Matt4:20), [MAT 4:21](https://ref.ly/Matt4:21), [MRK 1:18](https://ref.ly/Mark1:18), [MRK 1:19](https://ref.ly/Mark1:19), [LUK 5:2](https://ref.ly/Luke5:2), [LUK 5:4](https://ref.ly/Luke5:4), [LUK 5:5](https://ref.ly/Luke5:5), [LUK 5:6](https://ref.ly/Luke5:6)

Description:
------------

![Fish in a trammel net](../json/images/WEB-0336_trammel_net.png)

*Fish in a trammel net (Lindsay G. Thompson, University of Washington, CC0, via Wikimedia Commons)*

The trammel net was actually two or three nets combined in layers, usually a small\-mesh inner net sandwiched between two nets of looser mesh.

---

Usage:
------

Similar to the dragnet, the trammel net was set out vertically in the water, held in place by floats on the surface of the water and weighted down by a single weighted rope at the bottom. Fish swam through the outer layer and into the fine\-mesh net (1\), pushing it through the other layer of loose mesh (2\). This caught the fish in a kind of pocket from which they could not return (3\). Unlike the dragnet, which was drawn in immediately after it was spread out, the trammel net was left in place for several hours to give time for fish to accumulate in it.

---

Translation:
------------

The Hebrew words *matsod* and *mtsodah* probably refer to “net” in a generic sense, whether for fishing or for hunting.

In [MAT 4:20](https://ref.ly/Matt4:20); [MAT 4:21](https://ref.ly/Matt4:21); [MRK 1:18](https://ref.ly/Mark1:18); [MRK 1:19](https://ref.ly/Mark1:19); and [LUK 5:2](https://ref.ly/Luke5:2); [LUK 5:4](https://ref.ly/Luke5:4); [LUK 5:5](https://ref.ly/Luke5:5); [LUK 5:6](https://ref.ly/Luke5:6), it is possible that the Greek word *diktuon* refers to the dragnet (1\.3\.1\.2\), although the plural “nets” makes it likely that a trammel net is meant since it alone was made up of multiple nets. A generic term for “net” may be used in languages that do not require that a distinction be made.

* **Associated Passages:** Job 19:6; Ecclesiastes 7:26; Ecclesiastes 9:12; Matthew 4:20; Matthew 4:21; Mark 1:18; Mark 1:19; Luke 5:2; Luke 5:4; Luke 5:5; Luke 5:6
