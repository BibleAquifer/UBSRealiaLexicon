## Towel (id: REALIA:10.4)

10\.4 Towel
===========

References:
-----------

Greek λέντιον (lention)

[JHN 13:4](https://ref.ly/John13:4), [JHN 13:5](https://ref.ly/John13:5)

Description:
------------

The towel was a piece of cloth used primarily for drying. It would most likely have been made of cotton or linen (see [1\.5\.3\.7 Linen\<REALIA:1\.5\.3\.7\>](#)).

---

Translation:
------------

The particular piece of cloth in [JHN 13:4](https://ref.ly/John13:4); [JHN 13:5](https://ref.ly/John13:5) was large enough for Jesus to wrap around himself. The act of washing feet was normally performed by a servant or a slave. Such servants often performed their services dressed only in a loincloth (see [6\.4 Loincloth\<REALIA:6\.4\>](#)). While it may be good to explain this fact in a footnote, the translation itself will only be able to indicate it if there is a similar piece of clothing used by servants.

* **Associated Passages:** John 13:4; John 13:5

* **Associated ACAI Concepts:** Towel (ID: `realia:Towel`)
