## Straps (id: REALIA:6.13.1)

6\.13\.1 Straps
===============

References:
-----------

Hebrew שְׂרוֹךְ (srok)

[GEN 14:23](https://ref.ly/Gen14:23), [ISA 5:27](https://ref.ly/Isa5:27)

Greek ἱμάς (himas)

[MRK 1:7](https://ref.ly/Mark1:7), [LUK 3:16](https://ref.ly/Luke3:16), [JHN 1:27](https://ref.ly/John1:27), [ACT 22:25](https://ref.ly/Acts22:25)

Description and usage:
----------------------

Straps were long, thin pieces of leather. They were put to many uses, including tying sandals, binding other things (that is, used like rope; see [1\.14 Rope, cord\<REALIA:1\.14\>](#)), or wielding as a whip ([1\.1\.3 Whip, scourge\<REALIA:1\.1\.3\>](#)). See the illustrations at [6\.13 Sandal, shoe\<REALIA:6\.13\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

In a number of languages the Greek word *himas* may be rendered “leather string” or “strip of leather.” In [JHN 1:27](https://ref.ly/John1:27) the term may be absorbed within the total expression at the end of the verse, for example, “I am not worthy to untie his sandals” (implying the untying of the straps with which the sandals were bound). This may be done in [MRK 1:7](https://ref.ly/Mark1:7) and [LUK 3:16](https://ref.ly/Luke3:16) also.

In [ACT 22:25](https://ref.ly/Acts22:25) two meanings are possible for the Greek word *himas*. Luke may either be telling the reason why Paul was being tied up or the means by which Paul was being tied up. So translators may render the first clause in this verse as “they tied him up with thongs” or “they tied him up for whipping,” in which case *himas* would refer to an event, not to an object.

* **Associated Passages:** Genesis 14:23; Isaiah 5:27; Mark 1:7; Luke 3:16; John 1:27; Acts 22:25
