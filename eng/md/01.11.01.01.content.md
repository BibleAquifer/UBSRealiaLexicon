## Bellows (id: REALIA:1.11.1.1)

1\.11\.1\.1 Bellows
===================

References:
-----------

Hebrew מַפּוּחַ (mapuach)

[JER 6:29](https://ref.ly/Jer6:29)

Greek ζώπυρον, πῦρ (zōpuron tou puros)

[4MA 8:13](https://ref.ly/4Macc8:13)

Description:
------------

![Bellows operated by workers](../json/images/WEB-0368_bellows.jpg)

*Bellows operated by workers (© Le plombier du désert, CC BY\-SA 4\.0, via Wikimedia Commons)*

Where extremely high temperatures were needed, such as when smelting ore, it was common to blow air into the bottom of the fire with a bellows. One type of bellows consisted of two vertical cylinders covered with thick leather. The operator stood on these, pulling the leather up with an attached strap and then pressing it down with his foot to create the rush of air, which was channeled into the furnace.

---

Translation:
------------

Not all present\-day English speakers are familiar with the structure or operation of a bellows, so several common\-language English translations have avoided using the word in [JER 6:29](https://ref.ly/Jer6:29) by rendering the first line of this verse as “The fire is fanned to make it hotter” (NCV (New Century Version)), “The furnace burns fiercely” (GNT (Good News Translation (1992))), or “in a fiery furnace” (CEV (Contemporary English Version)).

* **Associated Passages:** Jeremiah 6:29; 4 Maccabees 8:13
