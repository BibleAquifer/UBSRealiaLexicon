## Finger (id: REALIA:11.4.1.1)

11\.4\.1\.1 Finger
==================

References:
-----------

Hebrew אֶצְבַּע (’etsba‘)

[JER 52:21](https://ref.ly/Jer52:21)

[JER 52:21](https://ref.ly/Jer52:21): In this verse some common\-language translations render the literal expression “four fingers” (RSV (Revised Standard Version (1952))) as “three inches” (GNT (Good News Translation (1992))), “seven centimeters” (SPCL (Spanish Common Language Version (Dios Habla Hoy))), “eight centimeters” (GECL (German Common Language Version (Gute Nachricht Bibel))FRCL (French Common Language Version (Bible en français courant))DUCL (Dutch Common Language Version)), or even “five centimeters” (Portuguese common language version). Older and more formal translations retain the “four fingers” of the original, and this is even done by some common\-language versions (ITCL (Italian Common Language Version)NOB). It seems likely that giving the thickness of the Temple columns as “four fingers” will communicate to the modern reader just as graphically as it did to the ancient one.
