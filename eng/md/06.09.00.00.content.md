## Garland (id: REALIA:6.9)

6\.9 Garland
============

Reference:
----------

Greek στέμμα (stemma)

[ACT 14:13](https://ref.ly/Acts14:13)

Description:
------------

The garland was a wreath of wool to which leaves and flowers might be added and either wound around a staff or woven into a circle to be worn on the head.

---

Usage:
------

![Wall painting from the west wall of Room L of the Villa of P. Fannius Synistor (Boscoreale, Roman, ca. 50–40 BCE)](../json/images/WEB-0504_garland.jpg)

*Wall painting from the west wall of Room L of the Villa of P. Fannius Synistor (Boscoreale, Roman, ca. 50–40 BCE) (Metropolitan Museum of Art, Public domain)*

Garlands were an important part of the ritual involved in the worship of pagan gods in the ancient world. As in [ACT 14:13](https://ref.ly/Acts14:13), the flowers were draped around animals to be sacrificed.

---

Translation:
------------

“Garlands” in [ACT 14:13](https://ref.ly/Acts14:13) may be rendered “circles of leaves,” “rings of flowers,” or “wreaths of flowers.”

* **Associated Passages:** Acts 14:13
