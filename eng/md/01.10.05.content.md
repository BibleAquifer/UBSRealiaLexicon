1\.10\.5 Anointing oil

Description and usage:
----------------------

Plain olive oil was used to anoint kings and sometimes prophets to dedicate them for their tasks.

The preparation of a special perfumed oil is commanded in [EXO 30:23](https://ref.ly/Exod30:23); [EXO 30:24](https://ref.ly/Exod30:24); [EXO 30:25](https://ref.ly/Exod30:25); [EXO 30:26](https://ref.ly/Exod30:26); [EXO 30:27](https://ref.ly/Exod30:27); [EXO 30:28](https://ref.ly/Exod30:28); [EXO 30:29](https://ref.ly/Exod30:29); [EXO 30:30](https://ref.ly/Exod30:30); [EXO 30:31](https://ref.ly/Exod30:31); [EXO 30:32](https://ref.ly/Exod30:32); [EXO 30:33](https://ref.ly/Exod30:33). It was for anointing the priests as well as the Tabernacle and furniture in the Tabernacle. It set them aside as “holy.” It had an olive oil base mixed with a variety of aromatic spices.

See also [1\.15\.1 Oil, ointment\<REALIA:1\.15\.1\>](#) and [9\.3 Olive oil\<REALIA:9\.3\>](#).

---

Translation:
------------

The anointing of a king was primarily a religious act, not a political one.
